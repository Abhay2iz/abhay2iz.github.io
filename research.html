AI and AI AGENTS

Large Language Models or LLMs are a type of artificial intelligence designed to understand and generate human language. They’re the brains behind tools like ChatGPT, Gemini, and LLaMA, and they’ve revolutionized how machines interact with text.

Key Traits Of LLMs

  • Despite being trained on vast amounts of data, they have limited knowledge of proprietary information, like our personal information or internal company data.
  • LLMs are passive – They wait for our prompt to respond.

AI WORKFLOWS

🧠 Understanding AI Integration Through App Access

If you ask a language model (LLM) about your next meeting, it won’t be able to answer unless it has access to the relevant data — like your calendar. To get accurate and context-aware responses, an AI assistant must be connected to the apps or tools that hold your personal information.

For instance, integrating your calendar enables the AI to check your schedule, while connecting to a weather service allows it to provide forecasts for that particular day.

Expanding access to additional tools — such as maps, email, fitness trackers, and smart devices — further enhances the assistant’s capability to deliver personalized recommendations, reminders, and even automate tasks in your daily routine.

📌 Related Enhancements Through Integration:

  • Calendar Access: Enables meeting reminders, schedule suggestions, travel time estimates.
  • Weather Data: Offers forecasts, clothing suggestions, and alerts for severe conditions.
  • Email & Messaging Apps: Drafts replies, summarizes long threads, flags priority items.
  • Smart Home Devices: Controls lights, thermostats, and appliances with contextual commands.
  • Maps & Traffic: Suggests routes based on current traffic and appointment location.
  • Fitness & Health Trackers: Adjusts recommendations based on sleep, activity, or goals.

🚀 Short Summary — What Are AI Workflows?

AI workflows are coordinated processes where artificial intelligence performs tasks by interacting with various data sources and apps. These workflows connect multiple systems to automate actions, provide context-aware assistance, and deliver smarter user experiences. Think of them as digital pathways where AI pulls data, makes decisions, and takes action — like a brain wired into your tools.

Note – Even when an AI system is integrated with multiple applications — such as calendar, weather, search engines like Google, or other tools — it still remains an AI workflow. Without autonomous decision-making or context-aware actions from an AI agent, there is no genuine agent involvement. It’s simply orchestrated access, not intelligent interaction.

🧠 Quick Contrast

Feature AI Workflow AI Agent
Task Execution Follows predefined steps Acts independently based on context
Decision-making Human-driven AI-driven
Adaptability Limited High
Example Analogy: Like pressing buttons on a machine (Workflow) vs. like asking a smart assistant to help (Agent)

RAG (Retrieval Augmented Generation)

RAG is a process that helps AI models to “look things up” before they answer. Essentially, RAG is just a type of workflow.

AI AGENTS

When we replace the human involvement from the AI workflow by AI models, then it becomes an AI agent. An AI agent must reason and act which is known as the ReAct Framework.
